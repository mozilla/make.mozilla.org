Overview

A Pop Up event:

  • Brings together local organizations
  • to host learning stations that participants can sample.

This guide will help! It's a community document, so please share your ideas to help improve it.

You can also see what other hosts have done.

Before

Engage local organizations
  • Research organizations in your city that might be interested in this event. Ideally, the participating organizations will share some core values, such as the learning by making ethic, or the role of out-of-school educational initiatives.
  • Talk to them about their interests, goals, and activities. How can this event help them?
  • Organizations agree to host a learning station, i.e. a simple table-top activity, based on their programs.
Craft good learning stations
  • Work with the organizations to plan good stations. Some recommednations:
    • Fun, short games or activities that teach you something about technology & other skills
    • Can be completed in 5min or expanded to an hour, as participants like
    • Participants can easily float in and out, on a rolling basis.
    • Participants can easily share what they did afterwards
    • Activities ar well documented and shared, for example on a website or wiki
  • Set aside time for organizations to see each other's activities and exchange ideas.
Find a space and a date
  • Find a comfortable, flexible space. You'll need several tables for each station and room to move about.
  • Think about wifi and power, good lighting, and snacks.
  • Pick a date. Give yourself enough time and make sure the date works for the organizations.
Spread the word
  • Write a short invitation, and create a page for your event,
  • Share the link widely, and encourage the participating organizations to invite their communities.
  • Read our guidelines on planning safe events

During

Welcome everyone
  • Set up tables in a large circle. Use posters or other signage to indicate the stations.
  • Do a quick go-round. Ask the station organizations to introduce their activities.
  • Give a brief overview. Explain how the stations will work and what's in store for the day.
Visit stations
  • Participants sample the stations.
  • Encourage them to follow their interests, either staying at stations as long as they like or moving on.
  • Remind facilitators to welcome newcomers and keep a good flow.

Share your hacks
  • After an hour or two, bring the group into a circle.
  • Celebrate what you made. Invite a few participants to share their work.
  • If you like, please share your hacks with the world!. You can publish the links to a blog or the social network of your choice.
  • Please tag your work with #mozparty.

After

Wrap Up
  • Ask everyone how it went. Encourage feedback about the event and the projects. What did they learn? What did they like? If they have suggestions for making it better, be sure to take notes or invite them to blog about it.
  • Talk about continuing to hack together. You may want to keep playing with these stations or to try new ones later. Talk about what the participants would like to do next, such as organize another event.
  • Set a date. If there's interest, pick a date for the next time!
Let us know how it went
  • Please share your experience about hosting the event and the projects, including links to the things you made.
  • We'll send an email reminder after the event to collect your feedback.
Getting Support
  • If you need help at any time before, during or after your event, please contact us. We have a team of volunteers and staff members dedicated to helping organizers like you.
